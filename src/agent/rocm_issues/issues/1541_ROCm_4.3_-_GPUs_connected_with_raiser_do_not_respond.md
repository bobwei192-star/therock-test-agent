### 评论 #27 — biergaizi (2024-09-04T14:32:34Z)

For the record (for people who comes here via search engines), there's an explanation to the original error, to my best knowledge.

    [drm:amdgpu_umc_logs_ecc_err [amdgpu]] *ERROR* Received I2C_NAK_7B_ADDR_NOACK !!!
    [drm:amdgpu_umc_logs_ecc_err [amdgpu]] *ERROR* WriteI2CData() - I2C error occurred :1
    [drm:amdgpu_ras_eeprom_init [amdgpu]] *ERROR* Failed to read EEPROM table header, res:-5

These errors are caused by missing I2C (SMBus) data signal on the PCIe slot of the PC motherboard - not all motherboards implement them. Apparently, I2C / SMBus signals are an optional part of the PCIe specification (see [1][2]), so many PC motherboards left them physically unconnected. But AMD Radeon VII-derived compute cards (Radeon Pro VII and MI50) appear to rely on I2C for RAS diagnostics (i.e. ECC status reporting). It's not a fatal error. Without I2C, ECC status can no longer be accessed or reported by the Linux kernel or ROCm tools - but they're not fatal, GPU itself still works. 

Because these GPUs are mainly designed for servers, it's likely why they depend on I2C. If you want to use these GPUs as they're intended, you need to find motherboards that implement I2C/SMBus signals on PCIe slots. Of all the PC motherboards I've tried, I only had success with one model.

After a recent kernel update, more I2C-related errors are now reported - they're still related to RAS and are not fatal, but surely they look scary to regular users.
